Private School Website Strategy

Parents choosing a school are making one of the most important decisions of their family's life. A private school website must balance academic credibility, community warmth, and practical information — and make it easy to schedule a visit.

Primary Audience

Parents of school-age children who are evaluating educational options — families new to the area, families dissatisfied with their current school, or families who have always planned to pursue private education. Both parents are often involved in the decision.

Primary Goal

Get a family to schedule a campus tour or request an admissions packet. In-person visits are where enrollment decisions get made. The website's job is to get the right family through the door.

Secondary Goals
  • Communicate the school's academic philosophy and approach
  • Show what student life looks like — not just academics
  • Provide practical enrollment information including tuition and financial aid
  • Serve current families with quick access to calendars, portals, and contact information
What Parents Care About
  • Will my child thrive here — academically and socially?
  • What is the school's educational approach and philosophy?
  • What does a typical day look like?
  • What are the outcomes — where do graduates go?
  • How much does it cost, and is financial aid available?
  • Who are the teachers and how long do they stay?
  • What is the community like? Will we fit in?
What Builds Trust
  • Accreditation and any relevant academic affiliations — stated simply
  • Teacher profiles with experience and tenure information
  • Student outcomes: college acceptance rates, test scores, or program achievements
  • Parent and student testimonials that speak to the community feel
  • Photos that show real students in real moments — classrooms, events, outdoor spaces
  • A clear, honest tuition page — even a range builds trust; hiding it creates doubt
  • An easy way to schedule a tour or speak with an admissions counselor
What Friction Must Be Removed
  • Tuition information that is completely absent or buried
  • Generic language about "excellence" and "developing the whole child" without specific examples
  • An enrollment process that is hard to find or requires too many steps to begin
  • Outdated photos that make the campus feel dated
  • No virtual tour or video option for families researching remotely
  • A website that serves current families well but leaves prospective families lost
Recommended Homepage Structure
  1. Mission or vision statement — clear, specific, and human (not generic)
  2. Primary CTA: "Schedule a Tour" — visible without scrolling
  3. Academic approach — what makes this school's philosophy distinct
  4. Campus and community photos — students in real moments
  5. Student outcomes — graduation rates, college acceptances, notable achievements
  6. Tuition and financial aid overview — even a "starting at" figure reduces anxiety
  7. Parent and student testimonials — specific and community-focused
  8. Admissions section — clear next steps and who to contact
Call-to-Action Strategy

The primary CTA should be "Schedule a Tour" — it is warm, non-committal, and matches how families move through the enrollment process. A tour is a conversation, not an application.

Offer a secondary CTA for families who are not ready for a tour: "Download Our Admissions Guide" or "Request More Information." This captures leads who need more time without losing them entirely.

What NOT to Focus On
  • Long lists of awards and rankings without context — parents want to know what it means for their child
  • Institutional history sections that go back decades — lead with today, not the past
  • Academic jargon or educational philosophy language that requires translation
  • A separate "prospective families" section that feels like an afterthought
  • Navigation so deep that parents cannot find admissions information quickly

"Choosing a school is an emotional decision wrapped in a practical one. Parents need to feel the community before they can evaluate the academics."

Why This Works
  • Choosing a school is an emotional decision wrapped in a practical one. Parents need to feel the community before they can evaluate the academics — and photos, testimonials, and honest language create that feeling online.
  • Tuition is the elephant in the room. Schools that hide pricing lose families who assume it is unaffordable. Transparency builds more trust than discretion.
  • The tour is the conversion. The website's entire job is to get the right family into a room with an admissions counselor — not to close enrollment online.
  • Generic language about excellence is everywhere. Specific claims — "85% of our graduates attend four-year colleges" or "our average teacher has been here 11 years" — are memorable and believable.
  • Parents research together and research repeatedly. A website that is easy to navigate on a phone at 10 PM, during the conversation about where their child should go, wins that moment.
